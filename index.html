Our Story

Welcome to Brew Brewery, your number one source for all things craft beer. We're dedicated to giving you the very best craft brews possible with a focus on flavour,diversity and price Founded in 2021 by Paul, Brew has come a long way from its beginnings as a home brewery. Brew had been a long-planned project of founder/owner/brewer Paul. Having been obsessed with beer and heavily involved in homebrewing for all his adult life, it was always an ambition to open a brewery or have a commercial brewing project that would allow him to produce a diverse range of beer. What began as a series of late-night kitchen table discussions is now a state-of-the-art brewery, producing an extensive range of premium craft beers.

Our Products

All our beers are brewed in small 2,500L so every batch gets the attention it deserves. We run our own bottling, canning and kegging lines in house and work hard to ensure the freshest beer reaches you.We’re very into what we put into our beers. We use hand-selected malts with different combinations for different brews. The same goes for our hops, where we select the very best from around the world to ensure they’re as fresh as can be and we get exactly what we’re looking for. We propagate our own yeast strains in-house so we know they’re consistent and unique to us.

Our Process

  • The first step in the production of beer is malting. Firstly the fresh barley gets soaked in water and put in the so-called germinating boxes to germinate.
  • The second step in the brewing process is milling. The finished malt is milled, similar to the making of flour, so that it dissolves better in water.
  • The third step in the brewing process is mashing. The milled malt is mixed with water in the mash tun – mashing-in.
  • The fourth step in the production of beer is lautering. The mash is filtered in the lauter tun as the husks sink and the wort is separated from the solid substances.
  • The fifth step in the brewing process is wort boiling. In the brewing pan or wort kettle the wort is boiled and the hops are added.
  • The sixth step in the brewing process is wort clarification or drawing off. Here the wort is fed into the whirlpool and starts to rotate.
  • The seventh step in the beer making process is the alcoholic fermentation. This takes place in a fermentation tank where special brewing yeast is added.
  • The final stage in the brewing process is the filling.
